Q1. First, could you please share your current designation & which department you are working under?
A: I am a Critical Care Consultant working in the Medical ICU (Intensive Care Unit).

Q2. On average, how many hours do you work in a week? How often do you have to work night shifts or extended hours?
A: I work around 6-8 hours per day, six days a week, and sometimes on Sundays. Night shifts happen once a week, and extra hours depend on the workload.

Q3. Have you worked under extreme conditions where you are working for over 24 hours without breaks? Do you think overworking affects your work-life balance? In what ways?
A: Yes, I sometimes work 24-hour shifts, especially on Sundays. Overworking affects work-life balance, but I try to maintain a balance between personal and professional life.

Q4. Do you feel that your workload has increased over time? If so, what do you think are the primary factors?
A: Compared to my residency, my workload has decreased in terms of physical labour, but there is more mental work and responsibility now.

Q5. Have you experienced any physical health issues due to OT (fatigue, weakness)? Mental health issues such as stress, burnout? How do you manage it?
A: No, I have not experienced any physical or mental health issues due to overworking.

Q6. In your opinion, does excessive workload impact patient care & decision-making? How?
A: Yes, in general, excessive workload can impact patient care and decision-making, but I have not personally faced such issues in my workplace.

Q7. Are there any policies in your hospital to limit/reduce OT? Are they effective?
A: Yes, the hospital has a structured shift system that prevents overworking. Shifts are confined to eight hours, and night shifts last from 5 PM to 8 AM. There are no prolonged working hours.

Q8. Do you feel comfortable discussing work-related stress or overload with your colleagues/superiors?
A: Yes, I can communicate with my superiors about workload issues, and they are supportive in adjusting shifts if necessary. There are no workplace politics affecting this.

Q9. Are there any counselling services/wellness programs to assist doctors? Does it help? Is it a good initiative?
A: No, there are no counselling services or wellness programs available.

Q10. What solutions/changes would you recommend to improve your working conditions?
A: The current structured shift system is already effective in preventing excessive overworking, so no major changes are required.
