Q1. First, could you please share your current designation & which department you are working under?
A: I am working in the General Medicine department.

Q2. On average, how many hours do you work in a week? How often do you have to work night shifts or extended hours?
A: I work around 50 hours per week. I do not have night shifts, but I do have extended working hours 1-2 days a week.

Q3. Have you worked under extreme conditions where you are working for over 24 hours without breaks? Do you think overworking affects your work-life balance? In what ways?
A: Yes, during training, I worked 24 to 36 hours without breaks. Overworking affects work-life balance as I am constantly on call, even during vacations, which makes it difficult to disconnect mentally.

Q4. Do you feel that your workload has increased over time? If so, what do you think are the primary factors?
A: No, I have set strong boundaries for my working hours. However, in some fields like surgery or gynaecology, workload naturally increases due to the need for emergency care.

Q5. Have you experienced any physical health issues due to OT (fatigue, weakness)? Mental health issues such as stress, burnout? How do you manage it?
A: Yes, overworking does impact both physical and mental health. The constant availability creates stress and fatigue. I manage it by setting work boundaries and delegating tasks when possible.

Q6. In your opinion, does excessive workload impact patient care & decision-making? How?
A: Yes, excessive workload can lead to exhaustion, which may affect decision-making and patient care. A well-rested doctor can perform better.

Q7. Are there any policies in your hospital to limit/reduce OT? Are they effective?
A: No, there are no specific policies for mandatory leaves or overtime restrictions. Doctors can choose to work as much as they want.

Q8. Do you feel comfortable discussing work-related stress or overload with your colleagues/superiors?
A: Yes, we do discuss it, but ultimately, it is an individual choice whether to take on more work or set boundaries.

Q9. Are there any counselling services/wellness programs to assist doctors? Does it help? Is it a good initiative?
A: No, there are no such wellness programs or counselling services available.

Q10. What solutions/changes would you recommend to improve your working conditions?
A: Implementing automation for certain tasks, restricting patient access to personal numbers, increasing manpower, and introducing a structured five-day workweek would help improve working conditions.
